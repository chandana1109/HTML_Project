Thailand

Thailand is a Southeast Asian country. It's known for tropical beaches, opulent royal palaces, ancient ruins and ornate temples displaying figures of Buddha. In Bangkok, the capital, an ultramodern cityscape rises next to quiet canalside communities and the iconic temples of Wat Arun, Wat Pho and the Emerald Buddha Temple (Wat Phra Kaew). Nearby beach resorts include bustling Pattaya and fashionable Hua Hin.
Capital: Bangkok
Official language: Thai
King: Maha Vajiralongkorn
Currency: Thai Baht
Dialing code: +66
Prime minister: Srettha Thavisin
Population: 7.16 crores (2021) World Bank.

  1. bangkok

    2. Bangkok is the capital and most populated city of Thailand. It's located on the Chao Phraya River delta, about 25 miles from the Gulf of Thailand.
    Bangkok is known for its palaces, skyscrapers, museums, and markets. It's also home to 49 colleges and universities, including Bangkokthonburi University, Rajamangala University of Technology Krungthep, and Chulalongkorn University.
    Bangkok is also known for its ornate shrines and vibrant street life. The Chao Phraya River feeds its network of canals, flowing past the Rattanakosin royal district. The district is home to the Grand Palace and its sacred Wat Phra Kaew Temple. Nearby is Wat Pho Temple with an enormous reclining Buddha and, on the opposite shore, Wat Arun Temple.
    Bangkok is also popular for temple-hopping, cocktails, and spas.

  2. pattaya

    3. Pattaya is a popular beach resort city on the Gulf of Thailand, about 150 kilometers southeast of Bangkok. It's known for its beaches, nightlife, and attractions that appeal to a wide range of people.
    In recent years, local authorities have improved the beaches and made Pattaya a more family-friendly destination. The city is lined with resort hotels, high-rise condos, shopping malls, cabaret bars, and 24-hour clubs. It also features several designer golf courses, some with views of Pattaya Bay.
    Pattaya has a wide array of attractions,including:
    Beautiful beaches and tranquil islands
    A nightlife scene
    Ancient Thai traditions
    Modern adventures
    Nature themed destinations
    Adventure filled attractions
    Family-friendly attractions
    Temples.

  3. phuket

    Phuket is Thailand's largest island and a popular tourist destination. It's known for its white beaches, turquoise waters, and warm climate. Phuket also offers adventure activities, unique culture, and excellent food.
    Phuket has many attractions, including:
    Beaches: Phuket has palm-fringed beaches, including Kata and Karon, which some say are among the world's best.
    Nightlife: Phuket has a vibrant nightlife.
    Cultural attractions: Phuket has iconic cultural attractions, ancient temples, and hidden caves.
    Elephant sanctuary: Phuket's first ethical elephant sanctuary provides a safe haven for sick, injured, and aged elephants.

  4. chiang mai

    Chiang Mai is a city in northern Thailand. It's the second largest city in the country, with a population of about 1.76 million people.
    Chiang Mai is a popular tourist destination and resort center. It's known for its Thai handicrafts, and nearby villages specialize in crafts like wood carving, silverwork, and lacquerware.
    Chiang Mai is also a cultural and religious center, and its Old City has hundreds of Buddhist temples, including the 14th-century Wat Phra Singh and the 15th-century Wat Chedi Luang. The Old City also has museums and other attractions.
    Other attractions in Chiang Mai include:
    Wat Phra That Doi Suthep
    Chiang Mai Night Bazaar
    Botanic Garden

  5. krabi

    Krabi is a province and resort town in southern Thailand. It's located on the Andaman Sea, where the Krabi River meets Phang Nga Bay.
    Krabi is known for its limestone cliffs, mangrove forests, and over 100 islands. Some of its most popular beaches include Railay Beach and the Phi Phi Islands. Other attractions include the Tiger Cave Temple, Ko Poda, and Khao Kanab Nam.
    Krabi is one of the most visited parts of Thailand, known for its beaches and national parks. It's also a popular starting point for island hopping to nearby islands like Koh Phi Phi and Koh Lanta.
    The best time to visit Krabi is between November and March, which is also the peak tourist season.

  6. hua hin

    Hua Hin is a beach resort town in Thailand's Prachuap Khiri Khan province. It's a popular tourist destination with many attractions, including beaches, mountains, temples, and night markets.
    Hua Hin is about 200 kilometers south of Bangkok and is considered a prominent coastal town. The town's name translates to "Stone Head" and comes from the rocks at the north end of its beach.
    Some say the best time to visit Hua Hin is between November and February, when the weather is pleasant and sunny. December and January are the most popular months for tourists, so it's recommended to book tickets and hotels in advance.
